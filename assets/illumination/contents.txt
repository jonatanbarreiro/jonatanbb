==============================================================================
 ILLUMINATION OF THE PAGE CONTENTS  (the text, not the logo)
==============================================================================

Companion to bb.txt. bb.txt is about the LOGO — how the two 'b's (and, by the
same points, the annulus) are lit. This file is about the other thing the
illuminating points light: the PAGE COPY — the running text, the headings and
the section rules of the site itself.

Introduced in iteration 18.


------------------------------------------------------------------------------
 1. TWO MODES OF LIGHT
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

A single illuminating point emits light in two distinct ways. Keeping them
separate is the mental model the code is built on:

  GLOWING (atmospheric).  The soft radial glow around a point, and — when the
      point is near the logo — the focused cones it throws at the annulus. This
      is the "air" around the light: how bright the space near it looks. The
      logo OCCLUDES this layer (a point behind the logo is blocked; near it the
      glow tightens into cones). Painted by main.js on the cast/reflect canvases.
      Kept at partial intensity, so it doesn't try to carry the whole effect.

  REFLECTIVE.  The light actually landing on a surface and lighting it up — the
      page text going gold where a point is near. This is the layer iteration 18
      built. It is INDEPENDENT of the logo: the logo does not block it, and a
      point in "cone mode" (close to the logo) still lights nearby copy. main.js
      hands this layer the point field ungated (each point's full wake w) and
      reflect.js renders it.


------------------------------------------------------------------------------
 2. HOW THE REFLECTIVE LAYER WORKS  (reflect.js)
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The page copy is real DOM text; it is never replaced or hidden. Over it sits a
fixed, full-viewport WebGL canvas that ADDS gold where the text is lit:

  - Each WORD (every heading and body word, wrapped in a <span class="litw">) and
    each section RULE (the black .kicker::after bars) is rasterised into a
    coverage mask at its own screen rect. Word rects and baselines come from real
    font metrics and are recached on any reflow (load / font-load / resize /
    browser zoom), so the gold stays registered with the glyphs at every size.

  - A fragment shader reads edge normals from that coverage and shades each glyph
    ink -> gold by how lit it is, from the point field (diffuse + a little
    specular, falling off over the point's reach). It is composited OVER the real
    text, so if the GPU ever drops the context the page just shows plain copy — it
    can never go blank — and the section rules and links keep rendering.

  - The raster is PINNED to the DOM, both ends of every word: the canvas is told
    the word's letter-spacing (canvas ignores CSS spacing by default — the cause
    of a gold shade drifting open along a long word), and whatever advance-width
    quirk remains, the raster is scaled onto the word's real DOM width. The gold
    cannot drift along a word.

  - The section rules are STRAIGHTENED b's (iteration 19): a bar as thick as the
    b's ribbon, wearing the b's top tip on each end — hook up on the left, hook
    down on the right (the 180° twin, like the monogram's two b's). Lit as three
    superimposed gold pieces — tip / bar / tip — with a hairline seam between
    them, so the joints read subtly, like the b's own middle joint. The straight
    run is rasterised not flat (a flat interior reads as a dead plateau to the
    normal-reading shader) but profiled: full coverage in the middle, soft
    shoulders at the long edges — stroke-like normals along the whole bar.

  - The about-photo FRAME is the same ink: as thick as the spine of the 'B' in
    Bastos, sharp corners. Rasterised as four mitre-cut sides (cut along the
    corner diagonals), each lit across its own thickness — four normals per
    side — with hairline seams along the cuts, so the corners joint like the b's.

  - The SELECTION OUTLINE (see §3) is ink too, and rasterised with everything
    else: the black box around selected text catches the points' gold the same
    way the words inside it do.

  - A visual word split over several spans by inline markup ("Barreiro" is
    Barre|i|ro around the i-dot anchor) is grouped: the pieces share one word
    identity, so it lights, clicks and selects as a single unit.


------------------------------------------------------------------------------
 3. SELECTION & CLICK
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The reflective idea extends to how text is picked out:

  - Selecting text turns the selected words gold and replaces the native
    selection highlight with a hollow, corner-rounded BLACK outline (one box per
    line).
  - Clicking a word toggles it gold. Either way the whole word turns — a grouped
    word (see above) never lights in parts.

The gold is the OFFICIAL site gold #fad02a (--gold, iteration 19): the shade of
a clicked word, of the annulus at max, of a fully lit letter. Partial
illumination everywhere mixes it with black along the warm ember ramp (incRGB),
never by fading gold over the pale page — that path washes the warmth out. The
ramp's low end is kept YELLOW (G tracking R closely): earlier stops leaned
brown/bronze in dim light and read olive on the b's beside a clicked word. The
GLOWING layer keeps its own colour (--glow #ffe666, deliberately cooler than
the gold — decided iteration 19), so the ambient air contrasts with the
reflective light.

A later milestone will let these lit (gold) words behave as light sources of
their own, casting reflective light on their neighbours.
