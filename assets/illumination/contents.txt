==============================================================================
 ILLUMINATION OF THE PAGE CONTENTS  (the text, not the logo)
==============================================================================

Companion to bb.txt. bb.txt is about the LOGO — how the two 'b's (and, by the
same points, the annulus) are lit. This file is about the other thing the
illuminating points light: the PAGE COPY — the running text, the headings and
the section rules of the site itself.

Introduced in iteration 18.


------------------------------------------------------------------------------
 1. TWO MODES OF LIGHT
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

A single illuminating point emits light in two distinct ways. Keeping them
separate is the mental model the code is built on:

  GLOWING (atmospheric).  The soft radial glow around a point — and nothing
      more. The glow is direction-free by design (iteration 22 dropped the old
      aimed cones — at the annulus, at the copy's bounding boxes — outright):
      the REFLECTIVE layer already shows exactly where a point lights, so the
      air around it no longer needs to point anywhere. Every point glows
      radially out to r₋ (the annulus' inner radius, its natural reach), with a
      physical falloff — a point lamp over a flat patch, a bright core and a
      soft tail. The glow is clipped OUT of the logo's disc, so no ambient air
      enters the annulus (this clip replaces the reach cap the cones relied
      on). The logo is still TRACKED by the points, but on one measure: a
      point's boundary/element lighting of the logo is gated by its distance
      to the annulus — full within one r₋ of the boundary, gone at 2·r₋ (the
      inner DIAMETER: a point entering the bowl sees the whole interior).
      Painted by main.js on the cast canvas. Kept at partial intensity, so it
      doesn't try to carry the whole effect.

  REFLECTIVE.  The light actually landing on a surface and lighting it up — the
      page text going gold where a point is near. This is the layer iteration 18
      built. It is INDEPENDENT of the logo: the logo does not block it, and a
      point close to (or inside) the annulus still lights nearby copy. main.js
      hands this layer the point field ungated (each point's full wake w, out to
      2·r₋ — twice the glow's reach) and reflect.js renders it.


------------------------------------------------------------------------------
 2. HOW THE REFLECTIVE LAYER WORKS  (reflect.js)
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The page copy is real DOM text; it is never replaced or hidden. Over it sits a
fixed, full-viewport WebGL canvas that ADDS gold where the text is lit:

  - Each WORD (every heading and body word, wrapped in a <span class="litw">) and
    each section RULE (the black .kicker::after bars) is rasterised into a
    coverage mask at its own screen rect. Word rects and baselines come from real
    font metrics and are recached on any reflow (load / font-load / resize /
    browser zoom), so the gold stays registered with the glyphs at every size.
    Registration is also SELF-HEALING (iteration 20): every frame, three sentinel
    words are re-measured against the cache — any drift (a zoom settling, a
    restored scroll, a late font) recaches and re-rasters within that frame. A
    recache always invalidates the raster, and the raster's cache key carries the
    zoom state (dpr + CSS viewport), because zooming in and back out can land on
    the same canvas size with a different world underneath — the cause of the
    proportional gold drift the iteration-20 zoom captures showed.

  - A fragment shader reads edge normals from that coverage and shades each glyph
    ink -> gold by how lit it is, from the point field (diffuse + a little
    specular, falling off over the point's reach). It is composited OVER the real
    text, so if the GPU ever drops the context the page just shows plain copy — it
    can never go blank — and the section rules and links keep rendering.

  - The raster is PINNED to the DOM, both ends of every word: the canvas is told
    the word's letter-spacing (canvas ignores CSS spacing by default — the cause
    of a gold shade drifting open along a long word), and whatever advance-width
    quirk remains, the raster is scaled onto the word's real DOM width. The gold
    cannot drift along a word.

  - The section rules are STRAIGHTENED b's (iteration 19): a bar as thick as the
    b's ribbon, wearing the b's top tip on each end — hook up on the left, hook
    down on the right (the 180° twin, like the monogram's two b's). Lit as three
    superimposed gold pieces — tip / bar / tip — with a hairline seam between
    them, so the joints read subtly, like the b's own middle joint. The straight
    run is rasterised not flat (a flat interior reads as a dead plateau to the
    normal-reading shader) but profiled: full coverage in the middle, soft
    shoulders at the long edges — stroke-like normals along the whole bar.

  - The about-photo FRAME is the same ink, rebuilt in iteration 20 as the bars'
    own material referencing the b's: a band one section-bar thick, each side
    wearing both b-tips hooked toward the exterior and, mirrored about the
    band's midline, toward the interior (poking subtly into the photo at the
    ends). Its dissection is Jonatan's, red-dotted onto the BW render: TWELVE
    exact non-overlapping pieces — 4 straight runs, cut off where each hook
    ends, and 8 corner PRIMITIVES, each a 7-cornered hook-end extended through
    half the corner square so the pair meets along the corner's full 45°
    diagonal (a solid joint; the first build's tips touched only at a point and
    read fragile). The small notch beyond each outer corner stays open. The ink
    is an SVG built by main.js (frameGeom, shared through LITAPI); the lighting
    is the same twelve pieces — runs profiled across the band, primitives
    solid, hairline seams at the cuts and along each diagonal.

  - The CONTACT ICONS (the Tuta mark, the Telegram plane) are line-art in the
    same ink, so iteration 20 rasterises their SVG paths into the mask too: a
    near point — or the ignition wave — lights them like everything else.

  - The SELECTION OUTLINE (see §3) is ink too, and rasterised with everything
    else in the photo frame's schematic: mitre-cut sides with corner seams. The
    gold layer sits ABOVE the outline's black divs (the divs live below the GL
    canvas) — with the stack the other way round the box's lighting is painted
    but never seen, which is exactly what iteration 19 shipped.

  - The IGNITION PULSE lights this layer as it travels (iteration 20): the
    shader carries the wave's tent envelope by each pixel's distance to the
    i-dot — rising from the announcer to the main front, draining to half at
    the requiem and to zero a trail later, the same three circumferences the
    logo reads. While rising the light rakes from the i-dot's direction and
    resolves to a flat, direction-free full gold as the front lands (the b
    elements' own treatment), and it is taken as MAX against the steady point
    light, so the wave lifts each glyph over its resting glow and hands back as
    it leaves. Cost: a few shader ops per masked pixel — no extra passes.

  - A visual word split over several spans by inline markup ("Barreiro" is
    Barre|i|ro around the i-dot anchor) is grouped: the pieces share one word
    identity, so it lights, clicks and selects as a single unit.


------------------------------------------------------------------------------
 3. SELECTION & CLICK
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The reflective idea extends to how text is picked out:

  - Selecting text turns the selected words gold and replaces the native
    selection highlight with a hollow, SHARP-cornered BLACK outline (one box per
    line — roundedness stays reserved for the annulus and the points). Across
    multiple lines only the selection's overall beginning and end are closed;
    an end where the stroke continues on the next line stays open, the box run
    a little past the text to say so.
  - Clicking a word toggles it gold. Either way the whole word turns — a grouped
    word (see above) never lights in parts.
  - Both golds belong to the INTERACTION layer, so the master light (the bulb)
    gates them (iteration 20): lights off, a click toggles nothing, a selection
    shows only the box (words stay ink — the gold is a CSS class the lights-off
    page mutes), and the contact icons no longer warm on hover.

The gold is the OFFICIAL site gold #fad02a (--gold, iteration 19): the shade of
a clicked word, of the annulus at max, of a fully lit letter. Partial
illumination everywhere mixes it with black along the warm ember ramp (incRGB),
never by fading gold over the pale page — that path washes the warmth out. The
ramp's low end is kept YELLOW (G tracking R closely): earlier stops leaned
brown/bronze in dim light and read olive on the b's beside a clicked word. The
GLOWING layer keeps its own colour (--glow #ffe666, deliberately cooler than
the gold — decided iteration 19), so the ambient air contrasts with the
reflective light.

Lit (gold) words as light sources of their own — casting reflective light on
their neighbours — was tried in iteration 22 and dropped in 23: a gold word is
copy, not a lamp. They keep their colour and cast nothing.
